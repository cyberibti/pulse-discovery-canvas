02 Key Users & Their World Today
IL
Ideation Lead
Topic sourcing & early validation
Identifies and shapes new venture topics. Runs early market research, builds the initial thesis, and drives topic validation before handing off to a Venture Architect/Manager. Currently working across scattered docs with no structured pipeline view.
No topic pipeline view Research in random docs No validation framework
VA
Venture Architect / Manager
Validation → Prototype → MVP
Owns the venture from validated topic through prototype (and optionally MVP). Architect or Manager based on seniority. Drives product direction, coordinates with PM for build, manages handoff to founders. Research and decisions live in scattered docs with no structured handoff process.
Research scattered in docs No structured handoff Decision log missing
PM
Product Manager
Prototype & MVP execution
Executes the product build — manages sprints, design, and dev during prototype and MVP phases. Coordinates with shared services. Currently uses separate Asana/Notion per venture — no consistency, can't compare progress across ventures.
Different tool per venture No standard process Sprint visibility
PF
Portfolio Manager
Venture health & governance
Tracks venture health post spin-out through Series A. Monitors KPIs, burn rate, milestones. Manages founder relationship and studio support allocation. Currently relies on monthly update emails and manual spreadsheets.
Manual KPI collection Stale data (monthly) No early warning system
SS
Shared Services Unit
HR · Talent · Ops · Finance
Provides cross-cutting support to all ventures — HR & talent acquisition, operations, and finance. Gets pulled in ad-hoc via Slack with no visibility into upcoming demand or workload across the portfolio. Critical during spin-out for entity setup and founder hiring.
Ad-hoc Slack requests No workload visibility Can't plan capacity
CEO
Founder — CEO
Post founder-match → Series A
Joins when the venture is ready to spin out. Needs to absorb months of context — research, decisions, product status, market data — in days. Leads company formation and growth with studio support. Currently gets a messy handoff of random docs, links, and verbal knowledge.
Lost during onboarding Context scattered No knowledge base
CT
Founder — CTO / CPO
Technical or product co-founder
Potentially joins alongside the CEO. Needs deep context on product decisions, tech stack, architecture choices, and user research. Often onboards even later than CEO, inheriting a codebase and product with minimal documentation of the "why" behind decisions.
No technical context pack Undocumented decisions No product history
03 Pain Points — What's Broken Today
Pain #1
Tool Sprawl & Fragmentation
Every venture uses a different combo of Notion, Asana, Google Sheets, Slack channels. No single source of truth. Context lost between tools.
All users
Pain #2
Zero Portfolio-Level Visibility
Leadership can't see which ventures are on track, which are burning cash, or where team capacity is going — without manually pinging everyone.
Ideation Lead Portfolio Mgr
Pain #3
Founder Onboarding is a Black Hole
New founders inherit months of institutional knowledge through ad-hoc meetings and scattered documents. Takes weeks to ramp up. High risk of lost context.
CEO CTO/CPO VA/VM
Pain #4
No Standard Venture Playbook
Each venture reinvents the wheel — different templates, different processes, different definitions of "ready to spin out." No repeatability.
VA/VM PM Ideation Lead
Pain #5
Profitability is a Guessing Game
No visibility into cost per venture, resource allocation efficiency, or studio-level burn rate. Financial data lives in separate spreadsheets maintained by finance.
Ideation Lead SSU (Finance)
Pain #6
Shared Services Overwhelmed by Ad-Hoc
SSU gets random Slack requests with no prioritization, no queue, and no way to plan ahead. Reactive instead of proactive.
SSU PM CEO
Pain #7
Handoff Gaps Between Phases
When a venture moves from ideation → build → spin-out, critical context drops. Decision rationale, failed experiments, and research insights get lost.
IL → VA/VM VA/VM → CEO
Pain #8
Status Updates are Manual & Stale
Weekly/monthly updates are manually compiled. By the time leadership sees them, data is old. No real-time pulse on venture health.
All users
Pain #9
Reactive Issue Detection
Struggling ventures are identified reactively, often when intervention windows have closed. Need leading indicators, not just lagging outcomes.
Portfolio Mgr Ideation Lead
Pain #10
No Institutional Learning
After 3+ years and 10–20 ventures, no pattern recognition for what distinguishes successful ventures from struggling ones. No benchmarking.
Portfolio Mgr VA/VM
Pain #11
Studio Ops Infrastructure Gap
Beyond venture tracking, the studio lacks operational infrastructure — knowledge management, people directory, workflow automation, and self-service tools.
SSU All users
04 Gaps — What's Missing from the Ecosystem
Gap #1
Venture Operating System
No unified system that tracks a venture from thesis through Series A with consistent structure, data, and workflows.
Gap #2
Stage-Gate Framework
No defined criteria or checklist for when a venture is ready to move from one phase to the next. Decisions are gut-feel.
Gap #3
Institutional Memory / Knowledge Base
No structured repository of decisions, learnings, research, and context per venture that persists across team changes.
Gap #4
Resource & Capacity Planning
No view of who is allocated where, what's the utilization rate, and where the bottlenecks are across the studio.
Gap #5
Service Request Workflow
No ticketing or request system for shared services. No SLAs, no queue, no tracking of what's been asked and delivered.
Gap #6
Founder Onboarding Runway
No structured onboarding journey for incoming founders — no "here's everything you need to know" in one place.
Gap #7
Decision Architecture
No defined decision triggers, thresholds, or governance for when to pivot, kill, or double down on a venture. Decisions are gut-feel.
Gap #8
Comparative Benchmarking
No way to extract cross-portfolio learnings or identify what distinguishes successful ventures from struggling ones over time.
Gap #9
Studio Wiki & Knowledge Hub
No centralized knowledge base with announcements, policies, playbooks, SOPs, and people directory for studio operations.
Gap #10
Self-Service & Workflow Automation
No self-service portal for common requests (employment letters, IT setup, visa requests) and no automated onboarding/offboarding workflows.
05 MVP Solution Scope — Pulse v1 Features
🚀
Venture Pipeline Board
P0 — Must Have
  • ✓
    Kanban pipeline view — Ventures as cards moving through lifecycle stages (Ideation → Validation → Build → Spin-out → Scale)
  • ✓
    Venture profile page — Thesis, team, stage, key metrics, decision log, linked docs all in one place
  • ✓
    Stage-gate checklists — Define criteria to advance between phases with approval workflow
  • ✓
    Health indicators — Traffic light status (on track / at risk / blocked) per venture
📊
Studio Dashboard
P0 — Must Have
  • ✓
    Portfolio overview — All ventures at a glance with status, stage, key metrics, and team
  • ✓
    Burn & budget tracker — Cost per venture, studio-level spend, budget vs. actuals
  • ✓
    Team allocation map — Who is on which venture, utilization %, availability
  • ✓
    Activity feed — Real-time updates across all ventures (decisions, milestones, blockers)
🗂️

P0 — Must Have
